3.1 TRANSPORT TIPS + TRICKS

Safe commutes to, from, and around uni campus

 WALKING + CYCLING

If you live close to campus, walking might be the best mode of transport for you. How can you optimise safety while travelling by foot?

History Lesson: In 1967, a student died after being hit by a car while crossing Stirling Highway - the second incident of the kind to occur within the past 2 years. Following this event, a push from students to gain a safer alternative to crossing the highway, resulted in the addition of several underpasses.

These underpasses are a great way to avoid the risk of crossing stirling hwy. The risk of walking at night time is also one to watch out for. UWA security provide an after-hours Escort Service and will walk you to car parks, colleges, and accomodation immediately adjacent to the University. To use this service, call 6488 3020. Help points with emergency phones are also located throught UWA campus.

UWA is a bike friendly campus so cycling is another great option for students living close by. Stay safe by wearing a helmet and watching out for other traffic, including pedestrians!

 PUBLIC TRANSPORT

Public transport is another option with several bus stops placed around the uni. Check out the table below to see what routes are available. Benefits of using public transport:

  • More time - no wasting time finding a car park
  • Cheap - with student fares and no need to pay for parking
  • Fun - can make the most of your spare time while being driven around
SmartRiders: while enrolled in UWA, you are eligible for a student concession Transperth SmartRider, which will allow cheap and easy journeys. You can use the Transperth website and app, or even Google Maps to see what is available near you.

 DRIVING

UWA have parking permits available to students who:

  • are studying full-time or part-time at UWA
  • have completed a minimum of 48 points towards a degree course
  • live far away from UWA campus
These parking permits cost $90 per semester and will allow you to park in the student bays around campus, which are indicated by yellow lines, whereas staff parking is red.

Tip: Park for free in the red and yellow bays on weekends, or after 5pm and before 8am on weekdays.

There is also paid visitor parking (green or white) for those not eligible for a permit. Click here for a map of the paid parking available around UWA.



REMEMBER: If staying late, UWA security is available to escort you safely to your car or residential college.

STUDY TIPS FOR UNI

Exams
Remember to:

  • Check which items are allowed in the exam
  • Bring:
    • Your student ID
    • A clear pencil case, carrying approved equipment
    • A clear water bottle
  • Turn your phone off
  • Have notes prepared in the case of an open book exam
  • Check student connect for the time and location of your exam

Time management
  • Pick up a UWA diary at Guild Student Central
  • Track classes and due dates with a diary or calendar
  • Break larger tasks into smaller ones
  • Make time goals for completing each task
  • Take breaks when losing concentrarion or to reward yourself after completing a task
  • Use your phone timer to stay on track
