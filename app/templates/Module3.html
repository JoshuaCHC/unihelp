3.1 TRANSPORT TIPS + TRICKS

Safe commutes to, from, and within uni campus

 WALKING

If you live close enough to the UWA campus, walking might be the best mode of transport for you. And this module can help you to optimise safety while travelling to, from, and around campus by foot...

History Lesson: In 1967, a 19 year old student died after being hit by a car on Stirling Highway. And this wasn't the first student fatality of the kind in those past two years. After this, students pushed for way to cross the highway safely, and this resulted in several underpasses...

...So make use of these underpasses to avoid taking the risk of crossing Striling Hwy. If you happen to be walking at night, UWA security provide an after-hours Escort Service and will walk you to car parks, colleges, and accomodation immediately adjacent to the University. To use this service, call 6488 3020. UWA also has safety help points located throughout the campus with emergency phones linked directly to security services.

 BIKES

UWA is a bike friendly campus so staying safe by wearing a helmet and watching out for other traffic including pedestrians is important!

 PUBLIC TRANSPORT

If you don't live close enough to travel by foot or bike, public transport is a great option! Some important benefits of catching public transport:

  • Don't waste time finding a park
  • Don't waste money paying for parking
  • Make the most of your tranport time: extra study time or just some relaxation
While enrolled in UWA, you are eligible for a student concession Transperth SmartRider, which will allow cheap and easy journeys. You can plan your trip on the Transperth website, Transperth mobile app, or even use Google Maps to see what public transport is available for your route.

 DRIVING

If the following apply to you, you may be eligible for a parking permit:

  • full-time or part-time student at UWA
  • has completed a minimum of 48 points towards a degree course
  • live far enough away from UWA campus
These parking permits cost $90 per semester. Different parking zones: red = staff, yellow = student permit Tip: You don't need a permit for red and yellow bays on weekends, or after 5pm and before 8am on weekdays otherwise there is paid visitor parking that might be indicated by green or white markings Click here for a map of the paid parking available around UWA. And again, if staying late, remember UWA security is always available to escort you safely to your car or residential college.

UniAccess teams provide support and assistance for students with a disability or illness affecting their studies - whether it be an ongoing, temporary, or episodic condition. Their role is to assist you in performing at your academic best, while keeping all information confidential. UniAccess can provide the following:
  • accessible course materials
  • special consideration
  • required equipment, technology
  • alternative exam arrangements
  • advice
